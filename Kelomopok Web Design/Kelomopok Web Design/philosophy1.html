Ayam Betutu khas Bali

In 1976, from hand processing, Ni Wayan Tempeh or Men Tempeh came from the Abiansi area, Gianyar city. Then, together with her husband named I Nyoman Suratna who came from Bangli, Ni Wayan Tempeh established a Ayam Betutu stall. The name Betutu itself comes from the words be and tunu, Be means meat, and tunu means grilled.

Kepiting Soka khas North Kalimantan

Soka crab or better known as mangrove crab is a food that is high in vitamins and protein. Like other marine animal products, crabs are also rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which is 407 mg / 100 grams. Omega-3s are very good for the brain. Giving from the fetus in the womb is very good for the process of growing brain cells and children's intelligence.

Papeda Kuah Kuning khas Papua

In this dish, there is a deep appreciation for nature and a strong connection with the surrounding environment by making wise use of local resources. Then, it reflects the importance of togetherness and social attachment in Papuan culture. Then, the process of processing sago into papeda requires extra attention and caution. It teaches the values of perseverance, thoroughness, and tenacity in the face of challenges in life. The importance of caring for and celebrating cultural heritage is passed down from generation to generation due to traditional dishes that have been around for centuries.

Es Laksamana Mengamuk khas Riau

Riau is an area rich in cultural and ethnic diversity. Ice Admiral Rampage may reflect this diversity in a simple and scrumptious way. Food is often a simple yet powerful way to celebrate cultural heritage and community values.

Bajigur khas Java

Bajigur is a drink made from a mixture of brown sugar, coconut milk, ginger, and coffee grounds or chocolate. This blend creates a unique balance of flavors, including sweet, savory, spicy, and bitter. It reflects the principle of balance in life and the important value of achieving harmony in everyday life. Suitable for the climate of Indonesia. It reflects care and attention to the health and comfort of the body, especially in colder weather.

Es Pisang Ijo khas Makassar

The green color of banana ijo is the hallmark of this dish. Green is often identified with the elements of nature, growth, and fertility. This reflects the expectation of good growth, both in terms of agricultural harvest and in human life. Ijo Banana Ice involves a meticulous process and hand skills in folding, rolling, and cooking it. It reflects creativity in food processing and expertise passed down from generation to generation. Importance of Tradition: Es Pisang Ijo is often associated with traditional and religious events in Makassar. It emphasizes the importance of keeping traditions and following social norms in society.

Es Degan khas Nusantara

Degan is a young coconut meat that symbolizes life and joy. The drink is very fresh and suitable for coping with hot weather. It reflects the desire to maintain freshness and vitality in life. The use of these natural ingredients reflects a strong connection to nature and sustainability. Then also, the ability to celebrate local identity.

Es Nona khas Pontianak

Es Nona is a creative food that generally consists of a mixture of shaved ice, jelly, milk, fruits, and various garnishes such as jelly and pearls. It reflects creativity in food processing and infinite variety that also emphasizes the uniqueness and diversity of domestic culture.

Es Goyobod khas Sunda

The process of making Goyobod Ice involves multiple layers, including sticky rice, green beans, and brown sugar, creating complex flavors. It reflects the depth and richness of the culture. Despite having various layers, Goyobod Ice is basically a simple and appetizing food. It reflects happiness in the simple things in life. Goyobod ice is often served in large portions and eaten together. It emphasizes the importance of togetherness and sharing in Javanese culture.

Lemper khas Yogyakarta

Lemper is a dish consisting of layers of sticky rice wrapped in chicken or fish fillings. The layers and contents that blend reflect balance and unity in life, as well as harmonious cooperation.

Klepon khas East Java

Klepon is a cake that has a brown sugar core covered by a layer of green sticky rice and a sprinkling of grated coconut. When eaten, brown sugar suddenly flows into the mouth, creating a sweet shock. It reflects creativity in serving dishes and fun in the unexpected.

Kolak khas Java

Kolak is a sweet dish made from bananas, sweet potatoes, and green beans cooked in coconut milk and sugar. Its sweet taste creates a sense of satisfaction and peace in the heart, and teaches appreciation for simple dishes.

Pisang Goreng khas Nusantara

Pisang goreng is a simple dish made from bananas fried in flour batter. It reflects delicacy in simple things and the importance of simplicity in life.

Kue Bingka khas Banjar

Bingka cake is a typical Malay dish that is often associated with Malay culture and traditions. It emphasizes the importance of caring for cultural heritage and preserving traditions.

Serabi khas West Java

Serabi is a pancake made from rice flour that often has a variety of flavors and toppings. It reflects creativity in food variety and the ability to create something unique.
